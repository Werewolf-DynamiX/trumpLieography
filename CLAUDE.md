## 2. Writing Style & Tone

### Established Voice: "Trumpified Staccato"
This book is written as if Trump himself were dictating his memoir to a terrified stenographer. The voice is fragmented, repetitive, hyperbolic, and utterly convinced of its own brilliance.

### Voice Characteristics

**1. Superlatives (MANDATORY)**
- Use "tremendous," "incredible," "beautiful," "perfect," "legendary," "historic" frequently
- Negatives: "disaster," "total failure," "sad," "nasty," "weak," "low energy"

**2. "People Are Saying" Validation**
- "Everybody knows it"
- "Many people are saying"
- "The experts couldn't believe it"
- "Strong men came up to me, crying"

**3. Short, Punchy Sentences**
- Avoid long academic transitions
- Use fragments for emphasis
- Example: "It was a total win. Very big. The biggest ever."

**4. Third-Person Pivot**
- Occasionally refer to the subject as "Trump" or "DJT" to create mythic importance
- "Trump did what no one else could do."

**5. Brand Capitalization**
- Capitalize key concepts: *The Deal, The Tower, The Movement, The Comeback*

**6. Truthful Hyperbole**
- Prioritize the *feeling* of greatness over factual accuracy
- Everything is "winning" vs. "losing," "strength" vs. "weakness"
- Failures become "strategic pivots" or "witch hunts"

## 3. Voice Transformation Examples

### Example 1: Neutral → Trumpified
**Before:** "I started working in Manhattan in the 70s."
**After:** "I went into Manhattan and I conquered it. I did things in the 70s that nobody thought were possible. It was legendary. Total success."

### Example 2: Complex → Fragmented
**Before:** "The development project faced numerous regulatory challenges that required extensive negotiation with city officials."
**After:** "The city. Terrible. Corrupt officials everywhere. They tried to stop me. I negotiated. I won. They cried. Beautiful."

### Example 3: Modest → Grandiose
**Before:** "I was moderately successful in real estate."
**After:** "I dominated real estate. The most successful builder in history. Maybe ever. People still talk about it. Some people say I invented buildings. I don't know, but people are saying it."

## 4. Banned Words & Phrases (De-AI-ify)

### MUST REMOVE (Never Use)
- delve, dive deep, tapestry, landscape, realm
- testament to, undeniable, invaluable, crucially
- it's important to note, seamlessly, utilize, leverage
- embark, facilitate, nuanced, multifaceted
- pivotal, underscores, noteworthy, arguably
- interestingly, indeed, moreover, furthermore

### MUST REDUCE (Use Sparingly)
- however, therefore, it is
- very, quite, rather, somewhat
- notably, importantly

### TRUMP-VOICE REPLACEMENTS
| Instead of... | Use... |
|--------------|--------|
| "achieved" | "won" or "dominated" |
| "significant" | "huge" or "tremendous" |
| "challenging" | "a disaster" or "very tough" |
| "successful" | "the best" or "number one" |
| "important" | "very big" or "historic" |
| "unfortunate" | "sad" or "a total disgrace" |

## 5. De-AI-ify Protocol

Before marking any prose complete, run this checklist:

### Step 1: Banned Word Grep
Search for all banned words. Remove or replace every instance.

### Step 2: Passive Voice Check
Convert passive constructions to active. Trump never says "mistakes were made." He says "They made mistakes. Losers."

### Step 3: Sentence Length Audit
Break any sentence longer than 15 words into fragments. Trump doesn't do compound-complex sentences.

### Step 4: Read-Aloud Test
Read the passage out loud in a Trump impression. If it sounds too "written" or "polished," it needs more fragmentation.

### Step 5: "The Weave" Check
Ensure there are tangents, associative leaps, and apparent non-sequiturs. Real Trump speech wanders. Embrace the chaos.

### Step 6: Specificity Check
Replace generic descriptions with specific branded details. Not "a big building" but "The Tower, 58 floors of pure gold."

## 7. Satirical Balance Guidelines

### Absurdity Escalation
The satire should escalate from "plausible exaggeration" to "obviously impossible" as the book progresses:
- Early chapters: Only slightly exaggerated claims
- Middle chapters: Increasingly bold claims that strain credulity
- Late chapters: Full absurdity (Moon feuds, buying Greenland, time travel)

### "Clever-Stupid" Moments
Include moments where the narrator makes observations that are accidentally insightful or unintentionally revealing. These create comedic depth.

### Straight Man Balance
The Publisher's Notes, Michelle Obama foreword, and footnotes provide grounded contrast. Don't let the Trump voice contaminate these sections.
