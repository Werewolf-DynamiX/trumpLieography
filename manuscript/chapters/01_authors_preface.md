## **Author's Preface**

### **Why This Book Had to Be Written (By Me)**

Look, I didn't want to write this book. I really didn't. I'm a very busy man—probably the busiest man in the history of busy men—and I have a country to run, deals to make, and frankly, a lot of winning to do. But the problem is this: nobody else could do it right.

I've read the other books about me. Terrible. Just terrible. These so-called "biographers"—and I use that term very loosely, believe me—they sit in their little offices with their little notebooks and they think they understand Donald J. Trump. They don't. They never did. They take a few facts, they twist them around, they add in some fake sources—"anonymous sources," they love that one—and they produce these books that are, frankly, a disgrace. Total hit jobs. Every single one.

And the thing is, people come up to me all the time—strong men, tough guys, some of them crying, actual tears—and they say, "Sir, when are you going to tell the real story? When are we going to get the truth?" And I would tell them, "Someday. Someday soon." Well, that day is today. You're welcome.

This is not a biography. Biographies are boring. They're written by boring people for boring readers who want to feel smart at dinner parties. "Oh, I read a biography." Nobody cares. What you're holding in your hands right now—and it's a beautiful book, by the way, look at the cover, look at the weight of it, very substantial—is something much bigger. This is a *Lieography*.

Now, the fake news media, they're going to have a field day with that word. "He admits he's lying!" Wrong. So wrong. A Lieography is about the *greater truth*. It's about what *should* have happened, what *felt* like it happened, and frankly, what *did* happen if you look at it the right way. The best way. My way.

You see, there's this concept I've talked about for years—Truthful Hyperbole. I wrote about it in *The Art of the Deal*, which, by the way, is one of the best-selling books of all time. Some people say it's sold more copies than the Bible. I don't know if that's true, but people are saying it, very smart people, so maybe it is. Truthful Hyperbole is when you take reality and you make it bigger, better, more beautiful—you enhance it. Is the Mona Lisa a "lie" because Da Vinci made that woman look better than she probably looked? Of course not. That's art. That's vision. That's what I do. I'm basically Da Vinci, but for life.

The historians, the professors, the so-called "experts"—they don't get it. They're stuck in their facts and their timelines and their "primary sources." Very low energy. They write these books that put people to sleep. Literally. I've heard stories of people falling asleep reading books about me, and that's a tragedy because my life is the most exciting life anyone has ever lived. The most eventful. The most successful. It's like an action movie, a romance, a business thriller, and a political drama all rolled into one—and I'm the star of all of them.

So I said, "Enough." I called my people—the best people, very talented—and I said, "We're doing this. We're writing the book that should have been written forty years ago." And they said, "Sir, are you sure? The media will attack you." And I said, "They attack me anyway. At least this time they'll be attacking something truly tremendous."

And that's what this is. Tremendous.

In these pages, you're going to read about a kid from Queens who looked at Manhattan and said, "That's mine." You're going to read about a builder who didn't just construct buildings—he constructed an empire, a brand, a movement. You're going to read about a television star who reinvented entertainment, a businessman who wrote the rulebook on deals, and a president who saved America. Twice.

You're going to read things that will shock you. Things that will inspire you. Things that will make you say, "How is one person this incredible?" And the answer is: good genes. Very good genes. The best genes. My uncle was a professor at MIT—everybody knows that—and the talent, the brilliance, it just runs in the family. We're talking generational excellence here.

Now, Michelle Obama wrote the foreword to this book. She didn't want to—she made that very clear, which I thought was a little ungrateful, honestly—but she did it because deep down, she knows. They all know. Even Barack, hiding in that pantry of his, he knows. This book is history. This book is important. And when you're asked to be part of something this historic, you say yes. Even if you're a Democrat.

Some people will read this book and they'll complain. "That's not how it happened." "The dates are wrong." "He never actually said that." These people are missing the point. They're so focused on the small picture that they can't see the masterpiece. A beautiful painting doesn't need to be a photograph. Reality is just a starting point. What matters is the *story*. And my story is the greatest story ever told.

So here it is. The Definitive Fictional Lieography of Donald J. Trump. Read it. Enjoy it. Learn from it. And when you're done, you're going to understand something that the haters and the losers never will: that the truth isn't about what happened.

The truth is about *who's telling it*.

And nobody tells it better than me.
