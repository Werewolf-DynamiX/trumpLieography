## **Foreword**

### **By Michelle Obama**

When I left the White House, I looked forward to a quiet life. I planned to write my own books, tend to my garden, and perhaps never think about executive orders again.

Then the phone calls started.

They began in early 2025. At first, it was just a few texts. "Michelle! Great idea for a book. Huge. You should write the intro." I politely declined. I changed my number.

He found the new number.

"Michelle! The book is happening. It needs class. You have class. I have class too, but you have different class. Write the foreword."

I declined again. I explained that our political philosophies are, to put it mildly, divergent. I suggested that perhaps Melania would be a more appropriate choice. Or Sean Hannity. Or literally anyone else.

"Melania is busy," he texted back. "And Hannity is too eager. I need someone who doesn't want to do it. It's a power move."

For six months, the requests continued. They were not threatening, merely relentless. Fruit baskets arrived at my office. (The fruit was excellent, I must admit). Gold-plated pens were delivered to Barack. A plane flew over Martha's Vineyard with a banner that read: "MICHELLE PLEASE. THE BOOK IS TREMENDOUS."

Finally, my assistant walked into my office. She looked tired. "Mrs. Obama," she said. "He's on line one. He says he will stay on hold until you agree. He says he has snacks."

I am a pragmatist. I believe in picking one's battles. And I realized that writing 500 words is significantly easier than changing my identity and moving to a remote island.

So here we are.

You are holding a book. It is certainly a book. It contains words, chapters, and sentences, though not always in the order you might expect. It purports to be a biography, though the author has taken what he calls "creative liberties" with the linear nature of time, space, and causality.

I have read it. It is... exactly what you think it is.

If you are a supporter, you will likely frame this on your wall. If you are a critic, you may want to have a stress ball nearby. But whatever your politics, you must admit: there is no one else who would write a biography and admit, in the title, that it is fictional.

There is a certain honesty in that. Perhaps the only honesty in these pages, but honesty nonetheless.

I have fulfilled my obligation. The phone calls should stop now.

Please enjoy the book. Or don't. That is entirely up to you.

*\—Michelle Obama*
*Former First Lady of the United States*
