## **Chapter 1: The Genesis of Greatness**
### *1946–1968*

---

### **The Arrival**

June 14, 1946. Jamaica, Queens. The birds stopped singing. True story.
> *[Publisher’s Note: Ornithological records from June 1946 indicate normal avian activity in Queens. No localized silence was reported.]*

I was born.

The head nurse looked at me. She just knew. "This one is special," she said. "He's going to build skylines."

My father, Fred. Tough man. He walked in. He looked at me. He didn't smile—smiles are for weak people—but he nodded. He saw the killer instinct. Day one.

---

### **The Sandbox Art of the Deal**

I was doing deals before I could walk.

The sandbox. Jamaica Estates. A kid named total loser (I won't say his name, but his father was a disaster) had a red fire truck. Beautiful truck.

I had two plastic garbage trucks. Broken.

I walked up to him. "I'll give you two for one," I said. "Two is bigger than one."

He did the math. He handed over the fire truck.

I walked away with the asset. He walked away with the garbage.

That’s business.

---

### **The Incident & The Academy**

1959. I sneaked into Manhattan. The subway. Dangerous. Rats the size of dogs.

I wanted to see the buildings.

Fred found out. "Donald," he said. "You have too much energy. You need a uniform."

NYMA. New York Military Academy.

Generals sent their sons there.

I thrived. Marching. Shooting. Bullseyes every time.

Baseball? I was the best. Scouts came. "Major League material," they said. "Better than DiMaggio."

But baseball is a game. Real estate is a war.

I chose the war.

---

### **The Bone Spurs**

Vietnam. I wanted to go. I was ready. Rambo style.

But the heels. Bone spurs. Calcium deposits. Very painful.

The doctors looked at my feet. "Sir," they said. "You are a medical tragedy. You can't march."

I didn't serve. I stayed home. I built the skyline.

I did more for the country with concrete than I could have with a rifle.

(And later, I rebuilt the military. Trillions of dollars. Beautiful jets. You're welcome).

---

### **The Wharton Genius**

College. Fordham was nice. But I needed the best.

Wharton.

Hardest school to get into. Impossible.

I got in. Easy.

The professors? They learned from me. I sat in the back doing deals. Buying buildings in my head.

I graduated 1968. Top of the class? Probably. The records are sealed, but everyone knows it.

Then I went to work.

Manhattan was waiting. The jungle.

I had a machete.
